# Daily Mood User Guide

Daily Mood is a private mood tracker and diary. This guide is for someone using the app for the first time. After reading it, you should be able to set up the app, record moods, review patterns, control your data, and adjust privacy settings.

Daily Mood keeps your entries on your device by default. It does not require an account, and your mood entries, notes, photos, voice notes, tags, and settings are stored locally.

## 1. First Launch And Privacy Lock

When you open Daily Mood for the first time, the app asks you to set a 4-digit PIN.

1. Enter a 4-digit PIN.
2. Enter the same PIN again to confirm it.
3. After the PIN is saved, the app opens the main screen.

Use a PIN you can remember. The PIN protects access to the diary inside the app.

After setup, Daily Mood can show the lock screen when the app needs to be protected again. On the lock screen:

- Enter your PIN to unlock the app.
- Tap **Use biometrics** if your device supports biometric unlock and you want to use it.
- If biometric unlock fails or is canceled, unlock with your PIN.

## 2. Main Navigation

The bottom navigation has five actions:

- **Home** shows today's check-in area, recent entries, trend status, and reflection streak.
- **Stats** shows charts and guided insights based on your saved moods.
- **Add mood** opens the quick mood logging flow.
- **History** shows saved mood entries with search and filters.
- **Setting** opens privacy, data, appearance, language, reminder, and haptic settings.

Tap **Add mood** whenever you want to record a new mood. It is an action button, not a separate tab.

## 3. Log A Mood

The quick log flow has four steps. You can close the flow at any time before saving.

### Step 1: Choose Your Mood

Choose the mood that best matches how you feel right now:

- **Awful**
- **Bad**
- **Okay**
- **Good**
- **Great**

The app uses this choice as the main score for charts, history, and insights. You must choose a mood before continuing.

### Step 2: Choose Emotions

Choose at least one emotion that gives more detail about the mood.

Examples include:

- For lower moods: **Angry**, **Overwhelmed**, **Sad**, **Anxious**, **Tired**, **Down**
- For neutral moods: **Neutral**, **Confused**, **Routine**
- For higher moods: **Calm**, **Satisfied**, **Stable**, **Excited**, **Proud**, **Energized**

Use the search field if you want to find an emotion faster.

### Step 3: Choose Reasons

Choose at least one reason or activity connected to your mood.

Default reasons include examples like **Work**, **Exercise**, **Social**, **Sleep**, **Nutrition**, **Family**, **Hobbies**, **Weather**, **Love**, and **Food**.

You can also add your own reason:

1. Tap **Add a reason**.
2. Type a short reason name.
3. Save it.

Custom reasons must be 20 characters or fewer. After a custom reason is created, it can be selected in future check-ins.

### Step 4: Add Notes Or Attachments

The last step is optional. You can:

- Write a private note.
- Tap **Add photo** to attach a photo.
- Tap **Add voice** to start recording a voice note.
- Tap **Stop voice** to finish the recording.
- Remove an attached photo or voice note before saving.

When you are ready, tap **Save**. If you do not want to add a note or attachment, tap **Skip and Save**.

After saving, the entry appears in Home, History, and Stats.

## 4. Home

The **Home** screen is the everyday view.

If you have no entries yet, Home shows an empty state and asks you to add your first mood. Tap **Add first mood** or the **Add mood** button in the bottom navigation.

After you save entries, Home can show:

- **Today's check-in**, including whether you have logged today.
- A small **Mood chart** for recent moods.
- **Weekly trend** status.
- **Reflection streak**, which shows recent check-in rhythm without requiring perfection.
- Recent mood entries.
- A gentle tip card.

Tap an entry to open its details.

## 5. View, Edit, Or Delete An Entry

Entries can be opened from Home or History.

In the entry detail sheet, you can:

- Change the mood score.
- Edit the note.
- Add or remove reasons.
- Add or remove emotions.
- Add, replace, or remove a photo.
- Remove an attached voice note.
- Tap **Save** to keep changes.

To delete one entry:

1. Open the entry.
2. Tap the delete icon or **Delete**.
3. Confirm the delete action.

Deleted entries are hidden from the dashboard and history.

## 6. History

The **History** screen helps you find past entries.

You can:

- Search notes, tags, or emotions.
- Filter by mood.
- Filter by date range: **All**, **Today**, **Yesterday**, **7 days**, or **30 days**.
- Tap **Clear filters** to return to the full list.
- Open any entry to review or edit it.

If no entry matches your search or filters, clear the filters or try another search term.

## 7. Stats

The **Stats** screen shows patterns from your local mood data.

Use the period selector to switch between:

- **Week**
- **Month**
- **Year**

Use the previous and next period buttons to move through time.

Stats can show:

- **Guided insights**, generated privately from your local mood patterns.
- **Mood Statistics**, showing how often each mood appears in the selected period.
- **Weekly Trend**, showing mood movement across the week.
- **Mood calendar**, showing mood patterns across a month.
- **Activity impact**, showing how selected reasons relate to average mood.

If you do not see a chart yet, add more mood entries. Some charts need enough entries before they become useful.

Guided insights are for reflection only. They are not medical advice.

## 8. Settings

Open **Setting** from the bottom navigation to manage privacy, data, and app preferences.

### Privacy Lock

Use **Lock now** to immediately lock the app. You will need your PIN or biometrics to continue.

The **PIN & biometrics** row shows lock setup information. Full PIN change controls are not part of the current screen yet.

### Export Data

Use **Export data** to create a readable backup file.

1. Open **Setting**.
2. Tap **Export data**.
3. Choose **JSON** or **CSV**.
4. Save or share the file using your device's share options.

JSON is better for app backup and restore. CSV is better for reading your entries in spreadsheet tools.

Photos and voice files are currently exported as relative path references. Keep this limitation in mind if you rely on media attachments for backup.

### Import Data

Use **Import data** to restore entries from a JSON or CSV backup file.

1. Open **Setting**.
2. Tap **Import data**.
3. Choose the backup file.
4. Wait for the import result.

After import, the app shows how many entries were added, updated, or skipped.

### Manage Custom Tags

Use **Manage custom tags** to control reasons you created yourself.

- **Archive** hides a custom tag from new check-ins.
- **Restore** makes an archived custom tag available again.

Archived tags stay attached to older entries, so your history remains understandable.

### Delete All Local Data

Use **Delete all local data** only when you want to permanently reset the app on this device.

This removes mood entries, notes, media links, and custom tags from this device.

To delete all data:

1. Open **Setting**.
2. Tap **Delete all local data**.
3. Read the confirmation message.
4. Type **DELETE**.
5. Tap **Delete**.

This action cannot be undone unless you previously exported a backup and import it later.

### Appearance

Use **Appearance** to choose:

- **System**, which follows your device setting.
- **Light**.
- **Dark**.

### Language

Use **Language** to choose:

- **English**
- **Tiếng Việt**

### Daily Reminder

Use **Daily reminder** to configure a local nudge.

- Turn the switch on or off.
- Tap the time to choose when the reminder should happen.

The reminder is intended to stay local to your device.

### Haptic Feedback

Use **Haptic feedback** to control light taps during mood logging.

- Turn it on if you like tactile feedback.
- Turn it off if you prefer a quieter logging experience.

## 9. Data And Privacy Notes

Daily Mood is designed for private, offline-first mood tracking.

- Your mood entries and notes are stored locally.
- Photos and voice notes stay in app-controlled local storage.
- PIN and biometric protection are handled through device security systems.
- The app does not require an account.
- The app does not automatically sync your journal to a cloud service.
- Exported files are controlled by you after you save or share them.

If your phone uses iCloud Backup, Google Backup, or another system-level backup, your device settings may include app data in those backups. That behavior is controlled by the operating system, not by Daily Mood.

## 10. Troubleshooting

If you cannot continue from the mood step, make sure you selected a mood.

If you cannot continue from the emotion step, select at least one emotion.

If you cannot continue from the reason step, select at least one reason or create a custom reason.

If a photo cannot be attached, choose a smaller image or try a different photo.

If voice recording does not start, check that microphone permission is enabled for the app.

If import fails, confirm that the selected file is a Daily Mood JSON or CSV backup.

If you want to keep a copy of your data before deleting or switching phones, export a backup first.
